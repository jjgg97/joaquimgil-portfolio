A Universe Made of Glass

Scent as
a Place in
Space.

This is my visual exploration of a fragrance — not as a product, but as a place. I imagined a universe where everything is made of glass: fragile, reflective, infinite.

Just like scent, glass is invisible until light hits it. It bends reality. It holds memories. It feels delicate — but it shapes space.

For this motion piece, I wanted to build a world around the Louis Vuitton Imagination fragrance — turning the bottle into a planet, the liquid into stardust, and the atmosphere into light trails of emotion.

The result is an abstract, floating dimension — suspended somewhere between luxury and dream. It's a place you can't quite touch, but you feel it. This is how I see scent: as a story told in light, reflection, and rhythm.

Context
Luxury brand storytelling through 3D motion

Louis Vuitton Imagination is a fragrance built on the concept of wonder and the unexpected. The challenge was to create a visual world that matched that ambition — surreal, refined, and technically precise — using Cinema 4D as the sole instrument.

Approach
The bottle as a universe

Rather than showcase the product conventionally, the bottle becomes a planet. The liquid becomes stardust. The glass becomes the atmosphere. Every element of the fragrance's physical identity is reinterpreted as something cosmic — intimate yet infinite.

Outcome
A surreal motion world in glass and light

A fully realised 3D motion piece capturing the emotional territory of the LV fragrance universe — material precision, editorial rhythm, and a luxury aesthetic that feels entirely new while remaining unmistakably Louis Vuitton.
