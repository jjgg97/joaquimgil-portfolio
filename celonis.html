My time as a full-time designer at Celonis in Munich was a formative experience in brand and professional growth. Immersed in the heart of a game-changing tech company, I had the privilege of not just executing design work, but truly learning how to shape, play with, and discover the nuances of a powerful global brand.

One of my key responsibilities was collaborating with the marketing teams on the renowned Celonis World Tour — a high-profile global event series requiring a high degree of agility and cross-functional communication. I worked with talented professionals from around the world, often juggling projects across five different time zones in a single day.

This fast-paced, high-stakes environment taught me to thrive on fast turnarounds. Working alongside seven other brilliant designers, I learned the value of diligent collaboration to meet every deadline and goal.

Context
A brand that must inspire and build trust

Celonis operates at the intersection of enterprise technology and operational transformation. Their brand had to simultaneously appeal to C-suite buyers, technical users, and global event audiences — without losing creative edge.

Approach
Walking the line between B2B and inspiration

Working directly with the Chief Brand Officer, the challenge was to walk the line between trust-building B2B credibility and genuine creative inspiration. Every asset needed to feel premium, human, and enterprise-grade simultaneously.

Outcome
Global campaign assets across 5 time zones

Delivered social media series, event posters, infographics for white papers, in-mail banners, and motion assets for the Celonis World Tour across New York, Paris, and multiple global cities — all under live deadline pressure.
