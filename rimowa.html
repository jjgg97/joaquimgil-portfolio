Luggage as Sculpture

Store
in Stone.

With the help of AI, I created a black and white world that reminds us of the artworks of Daniel Arsham — a luxury store carved into the wall. White is the predominant colour and nature's space is respected.

The Rimowa luggage is positioned inside the cave's spaces on a polished concrete floor. The customer is supposed to live the experience of discovering the favourite product and live the reality of purchasing a lifetime piece.

This project investigates how AI generation can serve as a design instrument rather than a replacement — using AI to explore visual territories that would be impossible to reach otherwise. Aluminum, concrete, stone and light as recurring motifs.

The result is an editorial brand world that feels entirely new while remaining unmistakably Rimowa: precise, durable, timeless. Luxury stripped back to its essential materials.

Context
How luxury brands can use AI without losing identity

The risk with AI-assisted brand work is generic output. The challenge here was to use AI as a precise instrument — directing it toward a specific aesthetic territory rooted in Rimowa's material codes and a clear art reference.

Approach
Daniel Arsham meets Rimowa aluminum

Arsham's signature erosion aesthetic — objects becoming stone, artefacts preserved in rock — provided the conceptual anchor. Rimowa's iconic aluminum cases reinterpreted as archaeological finds: precious, permanent, worth keeping forever.

Outcome
A brand world in stone, light and silence

A series of AI-generated brand images creating a coherent visual universe for Rimowa — a luxury retail environment carved from rock, monochromatic, silent and precise. Design as archaeology.

Visual Reference
Daniel Arsham

New York-based artist known for his "fictional archaeology" — everyday objects eroded into stone and crystal, preserved as if discovered centuries from now. His work bridges luxury, art and the passage of time.
