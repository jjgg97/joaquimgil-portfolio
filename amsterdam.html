The City of a Thousand Rivers

A Brand
Built on
Grid & Water.

The entire layout is built upon a strong, asymmetrical grid system reminiscent of De Stijl art. These lines define the primary structure and flow — not as static boxes, but as channels that direct the eye across the page, much like a river system carving paths through a landscape.

The brand concept centres on "The city of a thousand rivers" — Amsterdam's iconic canal network reinterpreted as a modern visual system. The canals become the grid. The grid becomes the brand. Structure and water as one.

Content blocks as land and water: the rectangular spaces created by the grid transform into solid blocks of colour or defined zones for text, imagery and interactive elements. Vibrant primary colours — red, yellow, blue — alongside black and white create visual weight and establish a bold, confident aesthetic.

Information areas use quiet, neutral spaces where typography and data are presented with clarity and focus — the inhabited parts of the city, where life happens within the structure of the canals.

Context
One of the world's most complex cities to brand

Amsterdam balances historic heritage, progressive values, international tourism and civic identity. A city brand must serve all of these simultaneously — speaking to locals, visitors, institutions and the global stage without losing coherence.

Approach
De Stijl grid meets canal geometry

Rooted in Amsterdam's Dutch artistic heritage, the De Stijl movement provided the structural logic. Primary colours, strict geometric grids and asymmetric balance create a system that feels both historically grounded and urgently contemporary.

Outcome
A flexible system for civic, cultural and commercial use

A brand architecture that works at every scale — from wayfinding and civic signage to social media motion graphics and editorial illustration. One system, infinitely adaptable, like the canal network itself.

Amsterdam City Brand — Identity system overview
Grid System
Structure as Identity

The asymmetrical De Stijl grid is not a container — it is the identity. Lines become canals. Canals become channels. The structure directs flow, rhythm and hierarchy across every application of the brand.

Colour Language
Primary Colours as Landmarks

Vibrant red, yellow and blue alongside black and white create visual weight, draw attention and establish a bold aesthetic. These are the landmarks of the city — unmistakable, confident, primary.

Content Areas
Land and Water

Solid colour blocks are the land — dominant, structural. Neutral white and grey zones are the water — quiet spaces where information flows with clarity and focus. The city lives in both.

Motion & Animation
A Living Brand

Motion graphics bring the canal-grid system to life. Lines animate like water. Blocks slide like lock gates. The brand lives and breathes across digital applications, social media and environmental contexts.
