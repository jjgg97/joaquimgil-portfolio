My Passage at SAP

I had the chance to collaborate within a multidisciplinary team at SAP's Barcelona HUB. Our mission: enhance the experience of the interns, maintain a well-preserved in-office culture, and ensure strong brand representation with partner universities.

My work consisted of internal communications — varying from videos to newsletters and design assets — collaborating on brand efforts and cohesiveness across teams. Keeping a good atmosphere for the humans in the office.

I collaborated in the creation of design assets for internal events such as Talent Day — an event thought to help interns network, know all the teams in the HUB and step out of their comfort zone in a fun and interactive way.

Some designs had to stick to brand guidelines, while others gave me more creative freedom. Created video communications experimenting with AI tools and Processing — a new creative fashion for enterprise internal comms. Also had the chance to collaborate in bold decisions, like the umbrella brand for the global Sales HUBs.

Context
Inside the world's largest enterprise software brand

SAP operates at unprecedented scale. The Barcelona HUB needed a designer who could bridge corporate brand standards with the energy of a young, dynamic intern community — without compromising either.

Approach
Brand discipline meets creative freedom

Some work required strict adherence to SAP brand guidelines. Other projects gave room to experiment — particularly with AI-assisted video using Processing, pushing the boundaries of how internal communications could look and feel.

Outcome
Events, identity and a stronger intern culture

Delivered Talent Day event assets, internal newsletters, AI video series, and contributed to the visual identity of SAP's global Sales HUB umbrella brand — all while actively contributing to team culture in the office.
