At SAP I had the chance to collaborate within a multidisciplinary team with a clear mission: enhance the experience of interns, maintain a well-preserved in-office culture, and ensure strong brand representation with partner universities.

My work consisted of internal communications varying from videos to newsletters and design assets — collaborating on brand efforts and cohesiveness across teams. Proactive engagement with team activities was essential to a successful performance.

I also had the chance to collaborate in bold decisions — like defining the visual identity of the umbrella brand for the Regions of the Sales HUBs across the globe. And I created a ton of video communications, experimenting with AI tools and Processing to present video in a new creative fashion.

Context
Inside the world's largest enterprise software brand

SAP operates at unprecedented scale — thousands of employees, partner universities, global Sales HUBs, and an intern programme that needed its own distinct internal brand culture. Design had to work across all of it.

Approach
Brand cohesion with creative freedom

Some designs had to stick strictly to SAP brand guidelines. Others gave more creative freedom — particularly for internal events and experimental video communications produced using AI tools and Processing.

Outcome
A stronger intern culture and global HUB identity

Delivered Talent Day event design, internal newsletters, video communications, and contributed to the umbrella brand identity for SAP's global Sales HUBs — balancing creativity with enterprise-grade brand discipline.
